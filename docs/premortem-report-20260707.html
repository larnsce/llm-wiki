Premortem — Issues #107 & #108

Source-gap triage + AI-transcript ingest route (#107) and model tiering (#108), both scoped for one session on 2026-07-08 — the last day of free Fable 5. Method: Klein premortem, 8 parallel investigators + adversarial counter-check. Run 2026-07-07.

Frame: It is 2026-10-07, three months later. Both issues shipped on the last free Fable day. Both have failed — the transcript route sits unused, the tier map was quietly abandoned. We look back and explain why.

Synthesis

Most likely failure

The implementable crowds out the expiring (reasons 1 + 8 + 9). Two canon-first spec PRs eat the day against the repo's verification bar; the Opus-vs-Fable trial — task 5 of 7, and the only workload with a hard deadline — is exactly what gets cut. The tier map's fable rows ship with zero demonstrations, unrepeatable at $10/$50 per MTok after midnight.

Most dangerous failure

Silent quality erosion (reason 7). Regenerating the goldens on Sonnet re-baselines the regression net to Sonnet's own calibration — after that, the tests can only confirm Sonnet agrees with Sonnet. Same-tier audit subagents confirm their own tier's errors; inflated reliability:: launders through the citation graph into durable wiki truth. Cheap to prevent tomorrow, nearly impossible to undo in October.

The hidden assumption

"Maximizing the last free Fable day means merging maximum tooling before midnight." Inverted by the counter-check: code is the free part — Sonnet can write every line of both issues on Thursday. The only thing that expires at midnight is Fable's judgment applied to real content: frozen golden baselines, a comparison corpus, hand-run transcript ingests, adjudicated Pending-Review items. The plan burns the expiring asset to manufacture durable ones, and lets the expiring one lapse.

Sub-assumptions never questioned: a transcript is a small clean markdown file; a checker of the same tier as the worker adds independent signal; a self-written log field measures execution rather than intention.

Failure cards

1 · Deadline-driven scope blowout P high D medium

Failure story

The tell was already in the CHANGELOG: 3.4.0 and 3.4.1 both shipped 2026-07-07 — the queue was being force-drained toward the deadline. On the day, #107's decision table was done by 09:00; the transcript route wasn't. The transcripts sensitive-type entry broke fresh-scaffold --strict lint at 13:40 (the scaffold gitignore template didn't know raw/chat-*.md); the fix rippled into setup.sh, storage.md, check_canon.py. The golden — the part that pins LLM judgment and can't be rushed — was still unwritten at 16:00. First tip: the harness ran in only one tool mode "to save time." Second tip, 19:15: the PR body's e2e transcript written from memory of a partial run. #108 got ninety minutes: two of four agents, no model field, trial verdicts pasted from a single unrepeated run.

Underlying assumption

A higher-capability model compresses the verification bar the way it compresses the writing — but harness, goldens, and e2e transcripts cost wall-clock time regardless of who types.

Early warning signs

Two releases tagged the day before the deadline (already visible) · any PR body whose e2e transcript timestamp predates the branch's final commit.

2 · Formalized before done by hand P high D med-high

Failure story

Every artifact assumed "a transcript is a markdown file you rename to raw/chat-*.md." The golden fixture was a hand-written, idealized 200-line dialogue — nothing real existed to fixture from, because no transcript had ever been ingested by hand. First real attempt: Claude Code /export produced a 41,000-line file of tool calls and diffs; the checkpoint blew context before reaching the design discussion. claude.ai had no clean per-conversation export; copy-paste lost the structure the REQ block assumed. The real workflow required an undocumented pre-trim verb the spec never named — while the golden kept passing against its fictional fixture. The voice pipeline had a Phase-0 exit test before its skill was built; #107 skipped exactly that gate to catch the deadline.

Underlying assumption

"Transcript" names an artifact like a voice memo — small, clean, self-contained — rather than an export-format problem never actually touched.

Early warning signs

Golden fixture authored, not captured — no real /export ever committed to tests/ · no Phase-0 manual-exit issue (the #57/#65 pattern) before the spec PR.

3 · Capture friction kills the habit P high D high

Failure story

Week one was novelty, not habit: two exports, July 9 and 11. The second checkpoint was the tip — re-litigating a decision made three days earlier, approve-then-opt-in, felt like being deposed about one's own conversation; twelve minutes to re-process something the brain had filed as done. The existing loop is one motion (drop, run, read); the chat route is five, and the first — noticing mid-conversation that this chat matters — has no trigger. Issue #65's gate A (10-of-14-days base review habit) was unticked when #107 shipped: a second habit stacked on an unformed first one. Zero transcripts after July 11; decisions from ~40 subsequent sessions evaporated exactly as before.

Underlying assumption

Valuing captured decisions would generate the five-step capture behavior — motivation substituting for a trigger.

Early warning signs

Zero new ingested transcripts in any 14-day window after week one · median chat-to-ingest lag exceeding 48h for the ones that do get captured.

4 · Gitignored provenance rots P low-med D high

Failure story

Transcripts instantly became the most-cited source type — 40+ pages by September, many carrying reliability:: low claims only a re-read could raise. The tip was invisible: from the first ingest, the bytes existed in exactly one place, and nothing complained. The vault's durability story is "git is the backup," and the gitignore surgically removed transcripts from it. Voice notes got REQ-1120 (off-machine archive.db copy before first real data, restore drills); transcripts got the gitignore pattern and nothing else — the commit records the move, not the bytes. Laptop migration in September: fresh clone, old disk wiped after git status came back clean. October audit: ENOENT on every transcript citation — 60+ claims unverifiable exactly where decisions live.

Underlying assumption

"In git history" and "durable" are the same property — so excluding bytes from git needs no replacement durability plan.

Early warning signs

git ls-files ingested/ diverging from ls ingested/**/* · fresh clone on a second machine immediately emits source-missing for all transcript citations.

5 · Triage agent structurally blind P medium D medium

Failure story

Week one it under-flagged: a preprint contradicting figures on an existing hub page hit triggers 1 and 2 — but Haiku could only see the queue file, not the wiki, so nothing looked like a conflict. Sonnet appended the new figure alongside the old; the contradiction hardened and three later ingests cited it as settled. "Is the target a hub page?" was equally unanswerable from the queue. Mid-August the prompt was "tightened" — when in doubt, flag — and an agent that could never evaluate its triggers was always in doubt: flag rate went ~4% → ~70%, Opus ran on grocery-list-grade captures, September's bill exceeded pre-tiering. Nobody caught either phase: no golden ever asserted "this fixture must flag, this one must not."

Underlying assumption

Escalation triggers defined in terms of existing wiki state can be evaluated by an agent whose inputs contain no wiki state.

Early warning signs

Sustained weekly flag rate <5% or >40% · any Sonnet-processed item later editing a hub/Schema page or changing a reliability:: value.

6 · Self-reported model log is fiction P high D medium

Failure story

Day one: the run-log spec told the executing LLM to write model <alias> — but a model can't introspect its ID, so the skill text effectively said "write the alias from the tier map." ~/.claude/agents/wiki-triage.md wasn't installed (setup.sh never re-run, scope mismatch); the Agent tool silently fell back to the session model — and the Dashboard line read model haiku anyway. Fiction from run one. At the July 21 review, /wiki-maintain status counted 31 haiku / 9 sonnet / 4 opus; the review concluded haiku triage had "zero regressions" (the session model had been doing haiku's job) and demoted two more tasks. The instrument measured the plan, so it validated the plan. The only cross-check — billing — was never in the loop; October's invoice showed premium spend flat against a log claiming 78% haiku.

Underlying assumption

The model executing a skill knows — or can be trusted to report — which model it actually is.

Early warning signs

Billing model-mix diverging from run-log counts (checkable in week one) · a 100%-clean model column with zero fallback/inherit entries — from self-report, itself the anomaly.

7 · Silent quality erosion P med-high D high

Failure story

It tipped at the golden re-review. Sonnet's re-run diffed against Fable-era transcripts and the diffs looked reasonable — reliability:: high where Fable had said medium-with-Pending-Review. The maintainer judged the new output defensible and regenerated the goldens, re-baselining the regression net to Sonnet's calibration: from then on the tests could only confirm Sonnet agreed with Sonnet. Structurally, audit verification ran on Sonnet subagents making the same characteristic errors as Sonnet ingest — one-origin sources counted as independent corroboration, hedges read as assertions — so audits returned clean verdicts on pages ingested with those same errors. Pending-Review sections quietly emptied. Because pages take the minimum across claims and later pages cite earlier ones, inflated ratings laundered upward through the graph. October spot-check: a drifted claim under reliability:: high, and no way to tell which stratum to trust.

Underlying assumption

Verification is a cheaper task than generation — a checker of the same tier as the worker adds independent signal.

Early warning signs

Rate of new ## Pending Review sections per ingest drops after the switch · golden re-run diffs cluster in reliability:: values, all in the generous direction.

8 · Opus-vs-Fable trial undoable as specified P high D medium

Failure story

The trial was task 5 of 7 in #108, behind the implementation, where it died. By evening, "re-run goldens once on sonnet" became the whole trial: no opus run, no fable run, no dense paper — none was ever picked, because "genuinely dense" was never defined. The deeper tip was scoring: the golden README's own rule — a diff is a re-review signal, not automatically a failure — means a diff can't produce a verdict. The one hasty comparison that ran produced two defensible checkpoint tables differing on a hub cross-link and one reliability rationale; adjudication was deferred to the July 21 review, which never happened. Weekly Fable sessions were never scheduled — nothing in #108 creates a trigger, only "session discipline" prose. October: golden headers say validated-on-sonnet; the fable tier rows shipped with zero demonstrations, re-runs now cost $10/$50 per MTok.

Underlying assumption

A regression net built to flag one model's drift can double, unchanged and unscored, as a cross-model benchmark squeezed into implementation leftovers.

Early warning signs

End of 07-08: no Fable verdict line in any golden header, no verdict comment on #108 · no dense-paper fixture committed before the trial day started.

9 · The scarce resource is misidentified P high D high counter-check

Failure story

All eight reasons critique how the two issues get built; none questions whether building them is what a final frontier-model day is for. The repo's own premortem states the governing insight: the plan engineers the free part (code) and assumes the expensive part follows. Specs, configs, skills, setup.sh edits are the free part — Sonnet can produce them next week at no urgency. What becomes expensive at midnight is Fable output: frozen golden baselines, the comparison corpus, a Fable pass over the real source gaps, Fable adjudicating the wiki's actual contested claims. That is precisely the data #108's map needs to be "validated by real data" — and the plan schedules it last, guaranteeing it gets cut. The plan burns the expiring asset to manufacture durable ones and lets the expiring one lapse.

Underlying assumption

"Maximize the last free day" = "merge maximum PRs before the clock," when it should mean "bank maximum irreplaceable model judgment."

Early warning signs

By midday, the session transcript shows only scaffolding and spec-writing — zero Fable invocations against real wiki or transcript content · nothing in either merged PR is an artifact only Fable could have produced.
