# Container-based sanitation

Container-based sanitation (CBS) is a service model in which excreta are
captured in sealable containers that are regularly collected, transported,
and treated — sanitation delivered as a subscription service rather than
as infrastructure.

## Key claims

- CBS is a full-chain **service model**, not a containment technology; evaluation belongs at the level of the [[wiki/concept/service-chain|service chain]]. — cite: [[notes/literature/@tilley2024|tilley2024]]
- Service reliability, not price, is the strongest retention predictor observed across programmes. — cite: [[notes/literature/@russel2023|russel2023]]
- ~~Containers require weekly collection to remain safe.~~ **superseded 2026-07-15** by `/wiki-update`: safe-collection frequency depends on container design and household size, not a fixed interval. — cite: [[notes/literature/@who2025|who2025]]
