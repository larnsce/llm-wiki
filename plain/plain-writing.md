A skill is a set of instructions the model follows. The page you are reading
explains the plain-writing skill in plain language. The model itself runs
the [standard version](../skills/plain-writing/SKILL.md), which the site
publishes word for word.
:::

## What it does

plain-writing changes how the model writes. The other skills in the suite
manage the wiki. plain-writing shapes the words in any prose the model
drafts or revises for you, such as documents, summaries, reports, and
emails. The rules ask for simple everyday words, complete sentences, no
dashes, no jargon, no analogies, and no filler. After drafting, the model
revises in two passes and removes every clause that does not add something
the reader needs. For a larger revision it can build a small HTML page that
shows what it changed and why, so you can review the edits.

The skill was written by [Shreya Shankar](https://github.com/shreyashankar/plain-writing-skill)
and is included here unchanged, under her MIT license. The plain language
pages on this site, including the one you are reading, follow its rules.

## When to use it

Use it whenever the model writes prose for you, and whenever you ask to
simplify, tighten, or clean up a text you already have.

## What it never does

The style is never applied to code, only to the words around it. A revision
may remove or rewrite sentences, but it never adds a claim that was not in
the text before.
