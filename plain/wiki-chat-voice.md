A skill is a set of instructions the model follows. The page you are reading
explains the wiki-chat-voice skill in plain language. The model itself runs
the [standard version](../skills/wiki-chat-voice/SKILL.md), which the site
publishes word for word.
:::

## What it does

wiki-chat-voice is a conversation with your recorded voice notes. It
belongs to the personal tier, so a default install does not include it. The
skill lists your notes with a short description of each, you pick the ones
you want to talk about, and it loads their transcripts. Then you have an
open conversation. You can ask what you said, compare notes with each other
or with wiki pages, and think out loud. When you say you are done, the
skill presents one closing checkpoint. It offers a short summary of the
conversation for today's journal page, and it offers each claim that could
go on a wiki page separately, with the exact sentences and the voice note
they came from.

## When to use it

Use it to revisit older notes, e.g., to pull together a week of recorded
thinking, or to find out what you actually said about a project.

## What it never does

Nothing is written while you talk, anywhere. The conversation itself is
never cited as a source. Every kept claim cites the underlying voice note,
and its trust rating stays low no matter how thoroughly you discussed it,
because a voice note records what you said, not what is true. The same
protection applies as in wiki-ingest-voice. What you said about another
person's private matters never leaves the transcript.
